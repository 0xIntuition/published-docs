Intuition Architecture Overview

Intuition is an ecosystem of technologies composing a universal and permissionless knowledge graph, capable of handling both objective facts and subjective opinions - delivering superior data for intelligences across the spectrum, from human to artificial.

Applications​

Intuition’s initial flagship application, Explore, allows users to create, navigate, aggregate, and curate attestations regarding people and things within the web3 ecosystem, a space where qualitative reputation and identity data is acutely lacking. Leveraging users’ social graphs (i.e., the people and organizations they “follow”) and distributed trust algorithms (e.g., EigenTrust), the application will be able to prioritize the display of relevant activity and tailor attestation weighting.

Middleware​

Although we’re building in-house applications to help demonstrate our infrastructure, our primary goal is to facilitate a healthy ecosystem of developers and partners integrating, building and experimenting on top of Intuition.

Our API & SDK allow developers to quickly replace existing functionality with attestations, adding to the visibility, utility and incentives associated with data that is already being created. E.g., a “thumbs up” in a product hunt for web3 app, or a “like” tied to content on a social platform can easily exist on Intuition’s open graph instead of within app-specific silos. The resulting data can then easily be queried and displayed within apps.

Infrastructure​

At the infrastructure level, Intuition remains completely unopinionated. We allow anyone to make any attestation about anything, incentivizing the creation of useful data, and then making it easy to navigate the resulting data. Users can search for a given person or smart contract to see what has been claimed or can alternatively query a set of tags to see a list of entities meeting those criteria. Intuition doesn’t opine on whether a claim is true or false, but rather displays what people are saying about a thing while providing tools to help analyze and filter.
